Risk Management Fundamentals

Risk 1–2% per trade

Professional traders rarely risk more than 1–2% of their account on a single trade. This keeps drawdowns manageable and lets you survive losing streaks.

Aim for 2R or better

A minimum 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio means you only need a 34% win rate to break even. At 50% win rate with 2R, you grow consistently over time.

Protect your capital first

Capital preservation is the #1 job of a trader. A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover. Use this calculator every time, no exceptions.

Leverage cuts both ways

High leverage amplifies gains but also losses. Always size your position based on your SL distance — not your account balance alone.

Consistency over perfection

One outsized loss can erase weeks of gains. Consistent 1–2% risk sizing across hundreds of trades compounds into edge — impulsive sizing destroys it.

Track every trade

Knowing your position size is only half the edge. Log every trade to discover which setups actually work for you — patterns emerge fast.

Frequently Asked Questions
How does position size affect my risk?
Your position size determines how much money you make or lose per price unit. By sizing your position so that your stop loss equals exactly your risk amount, you ensure every trade risks the same dollar amount — regardless of the stop loss distance. This is the foundation of professional risk management.
What's the difference between % and USD risk mode?
% mode calculates your risk as a fraction of your account (e.g. 1% of $1,000 = $10 risk). USD mode lets you set a fixed dollar amount to risk per trade regardless of account size. Most traders prefer % mode so risk scales proportionally as their account grows or shrinks.
What is a good risk-to-reward ratio?
Most professional traders target at least 1:2 (risk $1 to make $2). At a 1:2 R:R ratio, you can be right only 34% of the time and still break even. At 50% accuracy you grow steadily. A 1:3 or better ratio gives you a significant statistical edge over time.
Does leverage change my position size?
Leverage changes how much margin (capital) you need to hold a position, but it does NOT change your risk amount. Your risk is always defined by your stop loss. Using 10× leverage with a $100 risk still means you lose $100 if the SL is hit — but you only need 1/10th of the capital to open that position.
Why should I use position sizing?
Position sizing is the difference between sustainable long-term trading and blowing up your account. When you risk a consistent percentage on every trade, your wins and losses scale with your account. This allows you to survive bad stretches and capitalize on winning streaks without ever risking your trading capital.
